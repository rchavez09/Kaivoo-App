## Question

Is $249 the right one-time price for Kaivoo's Phase A productivity app? What does the competitive landscape actually charge, and what price point maximizes both revenue and user acquisition for the Phase B subscription funnel?

### 2. The One-Time Purchase Ceiling

Among productivity apps, **Things 3 is the high-water mark for one-time purchase pricing at ~$80 total** (across all platforms). No mainstream personal productivity app has successfully charged more than $100 one-time in the consumer market.

Apps that charge $100+ one-time are typically:
- Professional creative tools (Pixelmator Pro: $50, Affinity suite: $70-170)
- Developer tools (Dash: $30-80, Kaleidoscope: $150)
- Enterprise/B2B software (not consumer)

**At $249, Kaivoo would be 3x the most expensive one-time productivity app on the market, competing against products that are free or $5-20/mo.**

Sources: [Things 3 Pricing](https://richardriviere.com/things-3-pricing/), [Obsidian Pricing](https://obsidian.md/pricing), [Notion Pricing](https://www.notion.com/pricing)

### 3. Subscription Fatigue Is Real — One-Time Purchase Demand Is Growing

- One-time purchases are growing **6% year-over-year** as subscription fatigue increases ([Influencers Time, 2025](https://www.influencers-time.com/subscription-fatigue-in-2025-the-rise-of-one-time-purchases/))
- The average consumer manages **$33.58/month in total app subscriptions** and is reluctant to add more ([CC Creative](https://www.cccreative.design/blogs/how-much-are-users-willing-to-pay-for-app-subscriptions))
- Willingness to pay per individual app subscription: **$7-20/month** ([CC Creative](https://www.cccreative.design/blogs/how-much-are-users-willing-to-pay-for-app-subscriptions))
- The strongest one-time offers pair a "buy once" license with **optional paid upgrades, paid support, or add-ons** ([Influencers Time](https://www.influencers-time.com/subscription-fatigue-in-2025-one-time-buys-vs-subscriptions/))
- Over 60% of top-grossing apps now use **hybrid monetization** (multiple revenue streams) ([Foresight Mobile](https://foresightmobile.com/blog/mobile-app-economy-2026-monetisation-ai-foldables))

**Implication for Kaivoo:** The one-time purchase model is a competitive advantage — people actively WANT it. But the price must be in the range that triggers purchase, not sticker shock.

### 4. What Users Are Actually Replacing

Kaivoo Phase A (journal + tasks + calendar + captures + widgets) replaces:

| Replaced App | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Sunsama (daily planner) | $16-20/mo | $192-240/yr |
| Bear or Craft (notes/journal) | $2.50-5/mo | $30-60/yr |
| Todoist or Things (tasks) | $4-7/mo or $80 one-time | $48-84/yr |
| **Combined annual cost** | | **$270-384/yr** |

A one-time purchase that replaces $270-384/yr in subscriptions can justify a premium price — but the user needs to understand that math. The "replaces X subscriptions" framing is critical to pricing power.

### 5. Evernote: A Cautionary Tale

Evernote raised prices aggressively after acquisition by Bending Spoons — **over 70% increases for some users**. User backlash was severe. The free tier was gutted to 50 notes and 1 device. Users fled to Obsidian, Notion, and Bear.

**Lesson:** Users punish aggressive pricing when the value doesn't match. But they also proved they'll pay $10-20/mo for a tool they depend on — as long as it works well and doesn't insult them. ([Evernote Forum](https://discussion.evernote.com/forums/topic/156057-what-do-you-think-about-the-increase-in-subscription-prices/))

### 6. Indie App Success at Premium One-Time Pricing

- **Xnapper** ($29 one-time): $7,802 in early bird sales, ~$6K MRR at peak
- **DevUtils** (one-time, no subscription): Stable passive income, "cruising on auto-pilot"
- **Things 3** (~$80 total): Sustained success for 7+ years on one-time model

The pattern: successful one-time purchase apps price between **$29-80** and deliver clear, immediate value. None in the productivity space has broken through $100 successfully.

## Analysis

### The $249 Problem

$249 was set when Kaivoo's Phase A included more scope (self-hosted + AI features + full stack). For the current Phase A — a productivity app without AI — it sits in a dead zone:

- **Too expensive for impulse.** The app store / indie app buyer psychology has a ceiling around $50-80 for "I'll try it" purchases. $249 triggers "I need to research this for a week" behavior.
- **Too cheap for enterprise.** Enterprise buyers expect $249 to come with seats, SSO, support SLAs. A personal productivity app at $249 reads as overpriced, not premium.
- **Bad comparison math.** $249 = 2+ years of Notion Plus ($120/yr), 5+ years of Bear ($30/yr), or 12+ years of Amplenote Pro ($70/yr). Users will do this math.

### The "Replace Your Stack" Opportunity

The strongest pricing argument for Kaivoo is: **stop paying for 3-4 separate subscriptions.** If the average user pays $22-32/mo for their productivity stack (planner + notes + tasks + capture), Kaivoo at a one-time price saves them money within months:

| Kaivoo One-Time Price | Breakeven vs. $25/mo stack | Breakeven vs. $15/mo stack |
|---|---|---|
| $59 | 2.4 months | 3.9 months |
| $79 | 3.2 months | 5.3 months |
| $99 | 4.0 months | 6.6 months |
| $149 | 6.0 months | 9.9 months |
| $249 | 10.0 months | 16.6 months |

At $79, users break even in ~3-5 months. At $249, it takes 10-17 months — longer than most users will wait to feel the value.

### Volume vs. Revenue Tradeoff

The Vision.md target is 400 Phase A sales = ~$100K to fund Phase B development.

| Price Point | Sales Needed for $100K | Relative Difficulty | Phase B Funnel Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| $249 | 402 sales | Hard (price resistance) | 402 users |
| $149 | 672 sales | Medium | 672 users |
| $99 | 1,010 sales | Medium-Easy | 1,010 users |
| $79 | 1,266 sales | Easier | 1,266 users |
| $59 | 1,695 sales | Easiest | 1,695 users |

At $79, you need 3x more sales but build a 3x larger Phase B funnel. If 30% convert to Phase B at $29/mo, that's:
- 402 users × 30% = 120 subscribers × $29 = **$3,480 MRR**
- 1,266 users × 30% = 380 subscribers × $29 = **$11,020 MRR**

**The cheaper Phase A price generates 3x more recurring revenue in Phase B.**

---

## Recommendations

### Primary Recommendation: $79 One-Time (Phase A)

**Why $79:**
- **Market-aligned.** Just below Things 3's ~$80 total, which is the proven ceiling for one-time productivity apps.
- **Impulse-friendly.** $79 is in the "I'll try it" range for productivity enthusiasts. No week-long deliberation.
- **Clear value story.** "Replace your $20-30/mo productivity stack. Pays for itself in 3-4 months."
- **Volume-optimized.** Builds a larger user base for Phase B conversion.
- **Subscription fatigue tailwind.** "Pay once, own it forever" at $79 is a direct counter-narrative to the subscription market.

### Alternative: $99 with Launch Discount

- List price $99, launch at $79 (20% early adopter discount)
- Creates urgency and rewards early users
- Establishes a higher anchor for future pricing
- Still within the acceptable range for one-time purchase psychology

### Phase B AI Subscription Pricing (Preliminary)

Based on the competitive landscape, the AI subscription should target:
- **$19-29/mo** for the Concierge tier (AI assistant + integrations)
- **$39-49/mo** for the Builder tier (Workshop + marketplace + heavy AI)
- This positions below Motion ($29/mo) for the base AI tier and competitive with the premium daily planner market

### What NOT to Do

- **Don't go below $49.** Below this, the app feels cheap and the "replace your stack" math doesn't land — users think "if it's $49, it's probably limited."
- **Don't add a free tier for Phase A.** Free users cost infrastructure money and don't convert to one-time purchases. A 14-day trial is sufficient.
- **Don't bundle AI into the one-time purchase.** The whole hybrid model depends on AI being the subscription upsell. Keep Phase A clean: productivity only, no AI, no ongoing costs to you.

---

## Trade-offs

| Decision | Upside | Downside |
|---|---|---|
| $79 instead of $249 | 3x more accessible, larger funnel, impulse-friendly | Need 3x volume, lower revenue per sale, may signal "smaller" product |
| One-time instead of subscription | Subscription fatigue tailwind, differentiation | No recurring revenue from Phase A users who don't upgrade |
| No free tier | Every user is a paying user, clean unit economics | Slower top-of-funnel, harder to get first 1,000 users |
| 14-day trial | Users can evaluate before committing | Trial-and-churn risk, support burden for trial users |

## Open Questions (Require Founder Decision)

1. **Annual updates included or paid upgrades?** Things 3 includes free updates indefinitely. Some apps (Sketch, Dash) charge for major version upgrades. Kaivoo Vision says "1 year of updates" — is that the right policy?

2. **Platform pricing split or unified?** Things 3 charges separately for Mac/iPhone/iPad. Should Kaivoo be one price for all platforms, or split? (Recommendation: one price, all platforms — simpler, friendlier.)

3. **Early adopter / founding member pricing?** Should the first 200-500 buyers get a locked-in lower price or lifetime perks? This creates urgency and rewards the people who take a chance early.

4. **Refund policy?** One-time purchase apps typically offer 14-30 day refunds. What's Kaivoo's stance?
