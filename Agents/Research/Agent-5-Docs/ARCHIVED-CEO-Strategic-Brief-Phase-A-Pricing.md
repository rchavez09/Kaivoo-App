## Strategic Rationale

1. **Volume over margin.** Phase A exists to build a user base, not to maximize per-sale revenue. Every Phase A buyer is a warm lead for Phase B subscriptions.

2. **Impulse pricing.** $49 is dinner-for-two money. No week of deliberation. No "let me ask my partner." Users see the value vs. their $20-30/mo subscription stack and say yes immediately.

3. **Subscription fatigue tailwind.** "Pay once, own forever" is what the market actively wants in 2026. One-time purchases growing 6% YoY while subscription cancellations rise.

4. **Undercut the market.** At $49, Kaivoo is cheaper than Things 3 (~$80) while offering journal + tasks + calendar + captures + widgets. No competitor offers this much at this price point.

5. **No cap on founding members.** Founding member status closes naturally when Phase B launches and the price moves to $99. High volume is the goal — 5,000+ founding members is a great problem to have.

6. **Wide net for Phase B.** The entire purpose of Phase A pricing is to maximize the number of users available for Phase B subscription conversion. Even low-cost subscriptions ($4/mo sync, for example) become meaningful revenue at scale.

## Phase B Pricing: EXPLICITLY DEFERRED

Phase B pricing (AI subscriptions, multi-device sync, marketplace, collaboration) is **not decided** and requires its own research cycle before any numbers are committed. Key unknowns:

1. **AI token costs** — What does it actually cost to run the Concierge, Workshop, and smart routing per user per month? This determines the floor.
2. **Multi-device sync costs** — What does cloud sync infrastructure cost per user? Is $4/mo (Obsidian's price) sustainable, or does Kaivoo's richer data model cost more?
3. **Full Phase B feature set** — Pricing should follow functionality. We need to know exactly what ships in Phase B before we can price it.
4. **Competitive AI pricing landscape** — AI pricing is changing rapidly (Notion bundled AI into higher tiers, Motion charges $29/mo for AI scheduling). Need current data when Phase B is closer to launch.
5. **Founding member discount structure** — Can't set the discount until we know the standard price.

**Action:** Agent 5 to conduct Phase B pricing research when Phase B feature scope is finalized. Not before.
