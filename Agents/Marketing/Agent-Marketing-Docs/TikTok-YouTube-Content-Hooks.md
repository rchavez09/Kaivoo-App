## How to Use This Document

Each hook follows the Marketing Director's format:
- **Hook** (0-2 seconds) — The text/line that stops the scroll
- **Tension** (2-5 seconds) — Why this matters
- **Reveal** (5-25 seconds) — The payoff
- **CTA** (25-30 seconds) — What to do next

For YouTube, hooks expand into 3-8 minute scripts. For TikTok, everything is 30-60 seconds max.

## Production Notes

### Equipment Needed
- Good microphone (voice is the anchor. USB condenser or lapel mic.)
- Screen recording software (OBS, Loom, or native)
- Phone for b-roll (hands on keyboard, desk shots, coffee, screen close-ups)
- Basic editing app (CapCut or similar)
- Text overlay templates (hook text, key phrases, emphasis words)

### Recording Tips
- **Voice-over is the primary format.** Your voice carries the authority and personality. No camera needed.
- **Screen recordings for demos.** Narrate what's happening in real time or add voice-over in post.
- **B-roll for texture.** Hands typing, desk setup, whiteboard sketches, coffee being poured. Neck-down shots are fine. These humanize without requiring face.
- **Text overlays do the heavy lifting.** Every hook phrase appears on screen. Key stats, key phrases, punchlines. Works with sound off.
- **Raw > polished.** Authenticity lives in the voice, not the production value.
- **Batch record.** Record voice-overs for 3-4 TikToks in one session, then pair with screen recordings and b-roll.
- **Sound-off first.** Every hook works without audio (text overlays carry the message).
- **The face reveal is a future content moment.** When you're ready, it becomes its own event. No rush.

### Posting Cadence
- TikTok: 3-4x per week
- YouTube: 1-2x per week
- Twitter/X: Daily (mix of original + engagement)
- Reddit: 2-3x per week (value-first participation)
- LinkedIn: 1-2x per week
