## TikTok #2 — "The Blank Textbox"
**Thread:** What People Get Wrong
**Arc:** Vision (Present State > Bridge > Future State > Call-In)
**Post:** Wednesday

Every AI interface in the world starts the same way. A blank textbox. And the pitch is always: "It can do anything."

But if we've learned anything from UX, it's that people don't do well with an everything button.

Think about gaming for a second. Some people love open world games. They tinker, they experiment, they break things on purpose and find shortcuts the developers never imagined. That's what's happening right now with AI coding tools. People are using code editors to write presentations and build entire businesses. And that's amazing.

But what about the rest of us? The side-scroller players. The story-mode people. The ones who want a guided experience, not a blank canvas.

As a marketer with over 10 years in the game, I've seen this before. It's never one size fits all. Open world is great. But somebody needs to build the guided game too.

That's what I'm working on. A project called Flow. If you want to follow along, drop me a follow and check out the link in my bio.

**~50 seconds.**

## TikTok #3 — "Trim the Fat"
**Thread:** Build It Yourself / Death of the Application Layer
**Arc:** Builder (Challenge > Discovery > Solution > Share)
**Post:** Friday

I work in tech as a marketing executive. And once I really understood what AI could do, the first question I asked wasn't "what's cool?" It was "what can we stop paying for?"

Because software is built for the masses. And the masses model has a problem. Companies like HubSpot got huge by tying marketing to a CRM. Brilliant. But then they expanded into everything. And now you're paying a premium for a platform you use maybe 15 to 20 percent of. Because their other tools can't touch the specialized ones that are out there.

So what if you could build exactly what you need? A custom solution, tailored to your team, that replaces the bloated platform you're overpaying for?

That's happening right now. Early adopters are building their own internal tools with AI. Clean, focused, purpose-built. And it's giving them an edge nobody else has yet.

This is how people are going to thrive at work. Not by fighting the tools. By becoming the person in the room who knows how to build with them.

Give me a follow if you like this kind of thinking. And check out my project. We're launching April 16th and I'm excited to share a better way forward.

## TikTok #4 — "Where Does Software Go?"
**Thread:** Death of the Application Layer / What People Get Wrong
**Arc:** Wonder (Curiosity > Revelation > Excitement > Invitation)
**Post:** Sunday

Software is at such an interesting moment right now. Because my mind keeps going to the same question: where does it go from here?

Look at something like Copilot. You ask it to save a file and it tells you how to save a file. Meanwhile, AI agents that actually do things, that actually take action, those are the ones winning right now.

So if the entire movement is toward AI that can act, not just advise, then what happens to everything else? If AI can open a browser and shop for you, what's a mouse for? If AI can find any file with a simple prompt, what's the point of dragging through folders? If AI can write the document, why do you need the word processor?

We're not losing tools. We're outgrowing them. And what comes next is so much more interesting than what we have now.

My name is Ricky. I'm a marketing executive with over 15 years navigating the corporate world. I think about this stuff constantly. Give me a follow if you're curious too. And check out my own project where I'm trying to answer some of these questions.

**~50 seconds.**
